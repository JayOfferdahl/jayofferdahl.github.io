Portfolio


Hackfest 2016 (Confluency)

As mentioned above, Hackfest 2016 was a week-long hackathon put on by Cerner for the interns. My team, consisting of myself and three other KU undergraduates, took second place with our app: Confluency.

The purpose of Confluency is to track children's vocabulary development over time. Our original goal for the week was to connect out GUI/back-end with IBM Watson's speech to text API, which would allow us to take audio from a child's conversation, and convert it to text so we could perform analytical calculations on it in order to show a description of that child's progress. We ended up fleshing out the GUI/back-end to offer a proof of concept before the week concluded.

With this app, a user can view their children, each child's conversation history, as well as analytics on all conversations. Users can also record conversations and save them to a specific child.

We used the Ionic framework to build this application, which is built off of Angular.js and Cordova, and is made for mobile/web application development. For this project, I worked on both front-end and back-end, and spent quite a bit of time learning the Ionic framework.

In the end, I learned a lot about several of the packages we implemented, which consisted of django, python, bootstrap, and ajax, as well as more. I'm excited to continue developing this application for the future.

YourToDo isn't on a production server yet, but that's one of our primary goals for the future.
